## Introduction

        
One of the most complex financial decisions salaried individuals in India face every financial year is choosing between the Old Tax Regime and the New Tax Regime. The Indian government introduced the New Regime to simplify taxation by removing the clutter of deductions, but the Old Regime remains highly lucrative for those who actively invest in tax-saving instruments.

        
At Numeraise, we believe that optimizing your tax liability is the fastest way to instantly increase your wealth. In this comprehensive guide, we will analyze both regimes across different income brackets to definitively show you which one you should choose.

        
## Understanding the Old Tax Regime

        
The Old Tax Regime is the traditional system of taxation. Its defining characteristic is the availability of over 70 different exemptions and deductions.

        
- **Section 80C:** Allows you to deduct up to ₹1.5 Lakhs by investing in ELSS mutual funds, PPF, EPF, and Life Insurance.
            - **Section 80D:** Deductions for health insurance premiums.
            - **HRA:** Massive exemptions for House Rent Allowance.

        
## Understanding the New Tax Regime

        
Introduced as a default option, the New Tax Regime is designed for simplicity and higher liquidity. The tax rates are significantly lower across the board, and you pay zero tax up to ₹7 Lakhs. However, you must forfeit almost all deductions (80C, 80D, HRA). The only major deduction allowed is the ₹50,000 Standard Deduction.

## The Breakeven Point

        
The math reveals a fascinating trend. As your salary increases beyond ₹12.5 Lakhs, the New Regime mathematically dominates the Old Regime unless you have massive deductions like a massive ₹2 Lakh Home Loan Interest deduction.

        
If you have a high salary and you do not have a home loan, the New Regime is almost always mathematically superior.
