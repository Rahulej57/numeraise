5 Brutal EMI Mistakes That Will Cost You Lakhs

June 7, 2026 • By Numeraise Loan Experts

Introduction

Taking a loan—whether for a car, a home, or personal needs—is a major financial commitment. When the bank approves your application, they calculate an Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) that seems perfectly affordable. However, hidden within the mathematics of compound interest are traps designed to maximize the bank's profit at the expense of your wealth.

At Numeraise, we’ve analyzed thousands of loan amortization schedules. Here are the 5 most brutal EMI mistakes borrowers make, and exactly how much they cost you in rupees.

Mistake 1: Choosing the Longest Possible Tenure

When a bank offers you a loan, they will often stretch the tenure to the absolute maximum. Why? Because a longer tenure reduces your monthly EMI, making the loan feel "cheaper."

The Cost: Let's assume you take a ₹50 Lakh home loan at 9% interest. Over 20 years, your total interest is ₹57.9 Lakhs. But if you take a 30-year tenure, your total interest skyrockets to ₹94.8 Lakhs! By trying to save a mere ₹4,755 per month on your EMI, you end up paying the bank an extra ₹36.9 Lakhs in interest.

The Fix: Always choose the shortest tenure you can realistically afford.

Mistake 2: Never Making Prepayments

Your EMI consists of two parts: principal repayment and interest payment. In the early years of a home loan, up to 80% of your EMI goes entirely toward paying interest. The bank takes its profit first.

The Cost: If you never make prepayments, you will pay the absolute maximum mathematical limit of interest possible.

The Fix: Making even small prepayments directly reduces your outstanding principal, destroying the bank's ability to charge you future interest. Making just one extra EMI payment every year on a 20-year home loan will effectively close the loan in 16 years, saving you tens of lakhs.

Mistake 3: Flat Rate vs Reducing Balance Confusion

This is a very common trap in personal and car loans.

The Cost: A bank might offer you a "10% Flat Rate" loan. In a Flat Rate loan, interest is calculated on the original loan amount for the entire tenure, even though you are paying back the principal every month. A 10% Flat Rate loan is mathematically equivalent to a ~17% Reducing Balance loan! This trick costs you lakhs over the tenure.

The Fix: Never accept a flat rate loan without demanding the effective reducing balance rate first.

Mistake 4: Ignoring Processing Fees and Hidden Charges

Borrowers often obsess over the interest rate while ignoring the fees.

The Cost: A bank might offer a loan at 8.4% but charge a 1% processing fee plus mandatory insurance, while another offers 8.5% with zero processing fees. On a ₹1 Crore loan, a 1% processing fee is ₹1 Lakh immediately deducted from your pocket.

The Fix: Ask the bank for the APR (Annual Percentage Rate), which includes all hidden fees bundled into a single effective interest rate.

Mistake 5: Not Comparing Lenders and Negotiating

Loyalty does not pay in banking.

The Cost: Accepting the first loan offer from your primary bank means you miss out on promotional rates from competitors. Even a 0.25% difference in interest rate on a ₹50 Lakh loan over 20 years equals over ₹2 Lakhs in extra interest.

The Fix: Always shop around and bring competing offers to negotiate.
